About Bhadrachalam

Bhadrachalam, a sacred temple town in Telangana, is home to the famous Bhadradri Temple dedicated to Lord Rama. This ancient temple holds immense spiritual significance and attracts devotees from all over the world.

The temple is situated on the banks of the holy Godavari River, creating a serene and divine atmosphere for spiritual seekers. Bhadrachalam is also known for its grand celebrations during Sri Rama Navami, Vaikunta Ekadasi, and other festivals. The town is a gateway to spiritual exploration and cultural heritage, with legends dating back to the Ramayana era.

Nearby attractions include the scenic Papikondalu hills, the historic Parnashala (believed to be the place where Lord Rama spent part of his exile), and the tranquil Godavari river cruises. Bhadrachalam is not just a pilgrimage center but a destination for peace, devotion, and natural beauty.

Explore Around Bhadrachalam

1. Parnasala – 35 km from Bhadrachalam

A highly revered spot believed to be the place where Lord Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana stayed during their exile. You can see replicas of their hut and footprints, and the scenic Godavari river flowing nearby.

2. Abhaya Anjaneya Swamy Temple – on the way to Parnasala

A peaceful temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman, protector and devotee of Lord Rama. Devotees often stop here on the way to Parnasala.

3. Jattayu Paaka (Yetapaka) – 2 km from Bhadrachalam

Said to be the place where Jatayu, the divine bird, fought Ravana while he was abducting Sita Devi. A spiritually significant stop for Ramayana followers.

4. Gundala Hot Springs – 5 km from Bhadrachalam

Natural hot water springs surrounded by forest and nature. The water is believed to have healing properties, and it's a peaceful spot for a short visit.

5. Godavari Harathi Ghat – near Bhadrachalam Temple

In the evenings, a spiritual Harathi (aarti) is offered to the sacred River Godavari, similar to Ganga Aarti in Varanasi. A beautiful sight to experience.

6. Koonavaram – Around 35 km from Bhadrachalam

Located where the Sabari River meets Godavari, this is believed to be the place where Shabari offered fruits to Lord Rama. It's an important spot for pilgrims.

7. Dummugudem Barrage – 20+ km from Bhadrachalam

A scenic spot on the Godavari River, perfect for photography and relaxation. Popular with nature lovers and families.
